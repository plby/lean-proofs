\textbf{Summary:}
I plan to post an expanded and polished version to arXiv later. The claim is
that, for every sequence $(z_j)_{j\geq 1}$ on the unit circle,
\[
  \sum_{k\leq N} M_k
  \gg
  \frac{N^{5/4}}{\sqrt{\log N}},
  \qquad
  M_k
  =
  \max_{|z|=1}\prod_{j\leq k}|z-z_j|.
\]

Writing the logarithm of the product as a sum of the kernel
\[
  B(x)=\log|2\sin \pi x|,
\]
one first shows that a small terminal maximum $L_t=\log M_t$ forces all
low-frequency exponential sums of $x_1,\dots,x_t$ to be small. Fej\'er
smoothing with kernel $K_H$ and parameter $H$ then bounds each prefix maximum
by the interaction of the next point with the previous ones. Summing over the
prefixes converts this into a pair-energy estimate. Expanding that energy in a
Fourier series gives a lower bound for $\sum_{k<t}L_k$, up to an error
controlled by $L_t$. If $L_N$ is large, $M_N$ alone gives the result;
otherwise, choosing
\[
  H\asymp \frac{\sqrt{N}}{\log N}
\]
and applying AM--GM yields the stated bound.
